Intro:

Road Traffic Accidents (RTAs) are a major cause of death globally, leading to around 1.25 million deaths annually. Transport authorities worldwide have been striving to implement strategies to minimize RTAs by introducing various safety regulations. However, RTA reduction has proven to be an elusive goal as these measures have hitherto failed to make a considerable dent in the frequency of RTA.

This failure partially stems from the difficulty in predicting when and where RTAs may occur. Although difficult, such prediction is not impossible. RTAs do not occur in purely stochastic manner; several publications affirm that the probability of RTAs is influenced by multitude of measurable factors. These factors include obvious ones such as driving speed, traffic condition, road structure and weather to less obvious ones such as national mood and lunar cycle.

In an effort to reduce RTA frequency, various municipal and national governments in the USA have made available rich datasets of RTAs and their associated factors. We have explored these datasets and discovered factors that correlate well with RTA probability. Using this observation, we have created a machine learning model that predicts with high accuracy as to when and where RTAs are likely to occur.

Datasets and Research Methodology:

The main dataset we used for this project is the one retrieved from Kaggle. This is a countrywide car accident dataset, which covers 49 states of the USA. The accident data are collected Dec 2020, using multiple APIs that provide streaming traffic incident (or event) data. These APIs broadcast traffic data captured by a variety of entities, such as the US and state departments of transportation, law enforcement agencies, traffic cameras, and traffic sensors within the road-networks. Currently, there are about 3 million accident records in this dataset. Check here to learn more about this dataset.

US-Accidents can be used for numerous applications such as real-time car accident prediction, studying car accidents hotspot locations, casualty analysis and extracting cause and effect rules to predict car accidents, and studying the impact of precipitation or other environmental stimuli on accident occurrence. The most recent release of the dataset can also be useful to study the impact of COVID-19 on traffic behavior and accidents.

What day is road traffic accidents most likely to occur?

The plot shows that highest number of accidents occured Wednesday and lowest number are reported on Sunday.

On which weather, most of the accidents happened?

In the dataset, almost 128 differnent weather conditions are reported. From the plot, it is shown clear that most of the accidents occured when the weather was fair and second highest when the weather was clear.
